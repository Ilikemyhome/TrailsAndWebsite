What you might see along the trail!

Keep an eye out for:

Wildlife

  • Deers
  • Birds
  • Cayotes
  • Badger burrows
  • Uncommon - Black Bears and cougars

Vegitation

  • Timber rattlesnakes: Live in a variety of habitats, including pine forests, swamps, and river floodplains. In the western and higher altitude parts of their range, they are called timber rattlers
  • Eastern diamondback rattlesnakes: Live in pine and wiregrass flatwoods, coastal forests, scrublands, and barrier islands. They can also be found in abandoned farms and overgrown fields near pine forests
  • Pygmy rattlesnakes: Are fairly common in longleaf woodlands. Longleaf pine forests are among the most biologically diverse habitats in North America and provide quality habitat for many species
